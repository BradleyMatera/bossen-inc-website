Repair vs Rebuild

Not every problem needs the same approach. Here is how we think about it:

  • Repair: Fix the specific failed part and get the unit back in service.
  • Reconditioning: Clean, inspect, and restore components to reliable working condition.
  • Partial rebuild: Replace or rebuild a major assembly or section while keeping the rest intact.
  • Complete rebuild: Tear down and rebuild the unit to a fully operational, dependable condition.
  • Component replacement: Swap out worn or damaged assemblies with new, rebuilt, or salvage-quality parts.
  • Structural restoration: Repair or replace frame, panels, flooring, doors, roofs, and other major structures.

Potential Rebuild Work

We can evaluate and quote on major work such as:

  • Body panels and trailer skins
  • Trailer flooring and decking
  • Doors, roll-up doors, and door frames
  • Suspension, axles, and landing gear
  • Electrical systems and harnesses
  • Brake systems and air systems
  • Damaged structural components
  • Other major assemblies, subject to inspection

Our Rebuild Process

  1. Inspection: We assess the unit in person and document what needs work.
  2. Estimate: We provide a detailed estimate and timeline before any major work.
  3. Approval: You approve the scope, budget, and schedule.
  4. Repair or rebuild: We perform the work and provide progress updates.
  5. Final inspection: We verify the work before pickup or delivery.
  6. Pickup: You review the completed unit and hit the road.
